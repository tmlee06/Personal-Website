Winter quarter grades have come out, and it is not in my best interest to talk about them. I am very disappointed with myself to the point where I am disgusted to even look at myself in the mirror when I am brushing my teeth in the morning. It’s hard not to think of my first year of anything else, but a failure. I feel I wasted 22 weeks, two quarters worth of tuition, let myself, and others down, but those are my automatic negative thoughts and self talking. There are positives to take away from this year and I need to acknowledge them as well. For starters, you know the fundamentals, I learned how to live and take care of myself on my own, such as cook, clean, do laundry, time management, etc. I met some really smart and great people, and learned how to reach out for help. There are probably more positives, but those are just the ones that come to mind. Although I despise Kendrick Lamar, my first year was an experience, and I have to "accept the experience, appreciate the experience, and learn how to move on from things that don't serve you well."

I am working on the last part of the quote. Coming into UCSD, there was and still a lot of baggage that I have and been carrying around that I should and need to get rid of. Letting go is easier said than done, and finding forgiveness within your own self takes time, but it must be done. I do not know why, but I always felt and feel the need to constantly prove myself and had the bad habit of comparing myself to others. If anybody said the things I said about myself to a friend of mine, I would knock them out, but when it is my conscious talking, I just let it happen. This might sound strange and nonsensical, but subconsciously I always felt alone. There is a stark difference in being alone and feeling alone. It is not that there is nobody around you, it is that nobody has you. What I mean by "nobody has you" is that no one is holding the other end of the rope for you. You are just out there performing, competing, and proving your worth every single day, but if you slipped, you just hit the floor. It is the feeling that you are the only one responsible for your own survival. 

I do acknowledge that it is a bit of an exaggeration and to a nth degree I am being melodramatic in my writing because I did and do have a few people reaching out to me. But when you are in that head-space with those thoughts, you do not let people "have" you because you do not trust that they can handle the weight of the baggage you are carrying, and they have baggage of their own too. So, you keep everything all internal. You become a project to yourself instead of a person. In layman's terms, I am an engineer trying to fix a broken system from the inside, but I am also the system that is breaking.

You can call upon and talk to God, but He wants you to stay strong and go through your meaningful suffering to find purpose on the other side. Also like Jelly Roll said, 
"I only talk to God, when I need a favor
And I only pray, when I ain't got a prayer
So, who the hell am I?
Who the hell am I, to expect a savior?"

Our relationship with God or with anyone cannot be convenient and transactional. As I am talking about God right now, I always loved this verse in the Bible, and tried applying it to my life, but there have been more failures than successes in doing so. 

Ecclesiastes 4:11
"Also, if two lie down together, they will keep warm. But how can one keep warm alone?"

For context, Ecclesiastes is a raw, intellectual look at the meaning of life from someone who had everything and still felt empty. The obvious interpretation of the verse is that in the ancient world, travelers in the desert or mountains faced nights where temperatures dropped below freezing. They did not have high-tech gear or insulated sleeping bags, sharing a blanket and body heat was a mechanical necessity. If you were alone, you risked hypothermia. I interpret the verse as a metaphor for the fact that humans are not designed to be self-sustaining. We need a 'tether' or a witness to help us maintain our internal fire when the world starts to feel cold or indifferent. I always loved this quote stemming from Harlow James: "Everyone wants to be someone’s sun, to light up someone’s life, but why not the moon, to shine on someone's darkest hour."

In no way I am a moon, but I feel like the man on the moon. That is probably the worst attempted banger for a play on words, but it is whatever. I felt like such a fraud and failure. Feeling so undeserving of every privilege and everything I had. There was so much regret and anger, I disrespected everyone who did not get into the school let alone the ECE program. Everyone told me to have grace, stop being so hard on myself, but I never really knew what it meant. I went to bed that night prepared to live with my sorrows and be an even more insufferable person than I was for the next six months and until I get back to La Jolla to do it all over again. Insanity is doing the same thing over and over again expecting a different result, but my pride and ego was not going to let me change majors and not take the course again. I wanted to do things my way, but did not even know where to begin or what direction I was heading, I lost all confidence in myself. The next day, when I went to check my portal to make sure my tuition for study abroad was paid and everything was good; I decided to check my grades and see the damage that has been done. I was prepared to see my GPA lower than my previous of 1.43, but it wasn’t. I saw 2.32. My final grades of Winter quarter were B, C+, and C. Obviously I was saved by a gigantic curve, but most importantly I was saved by God’s grace. There was no reason why I should have passed two of my courses, but I did. Why me? God really answered my favor, and I am forever thankful. I had to turn to Romans 8:31 right away.

Romans 8:31
“What shall we then say to these things? If God be for us, who can be against us?”

Although the GPA is still abysmal, with God I am playing to win here. I was saved by his grace, and everyone is. 

Ephesians 2:8-9
“For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: not of works, lest any man should boast.”

Grace is undeserved kindness, forgiveness, and poise. Life is not at all transactional, and it is not measured in accomplishments, accolades, titles, numbers, and resumes, but moments. Like that famous quote, "Life is not measured by the number of breaths we take, but by the moments that take our breath away,” and I have been fortunate enough to have so many. My eyes are rich to see the beautiful landscapes of the world whenever I travel, live through my darkest moments, and spend time with the great people around me. As I type this under a Sakura tree, I think back to King David in Psalms 8:3-4.

Psalm 8:3-4:

“When I consider thy heavens, the work of thy fingers, The moon and the stars, which thou hast ordained; “What is man, that thou art mindful of him? And the son of man, that thou visitest him?”

We are meant to be here too. We are loved and we are saved. Tristan or anybody, comeback to this reflection and remember everything is going to be alright, and we got through this difficult time in our life, and with God and the great people around us, we are bounded by nothing. I love you and I love you all who read. Keep putting one foot in front of the other, trust God, and let everything fall into its right place.
