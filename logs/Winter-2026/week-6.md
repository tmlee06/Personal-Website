If the Olympics were not on, I have no clue how I am getting through Week 6. With no Olympics and NFL being over, how did students in previous years get through midterm week? I give a lot of respect to them, it takes a lot of courage to get bombarded with exams and having nothing to look forward too. Fortunately for me that was not the case. Olympics were on, I got to go LA this weekend, life was not bad, even though this was a rough week.

Monday was one of the best Monday I had in a while. I got to finally comeback and workout in the D1 gym. Granted I was moving some sorry weight, it was still nice to get a sweat in after two weeks of inactivity. My lectures and discussions went pretty smooth. I went to review my circuits quiz in office hours to see where I went wrong, and it was exactly what I thought I did. Silly mistakes. Even the professor chewed me out on it, but he said he's happy to see me doing better this time around. I need to figure out how to avoid making silly mistakes because that has been the story of the quarter so far. The lights are always too bright for me, I guess. I also reviewed my Data Science quiz too, which sucked because my TA told me my logic and reasoning is correct, but my silly mistakes costed me. I just have test-taking and performance anxiety, I need to overcome.

Data science midterm ate me up, straight failure, I am below the mean and the median. I tried to convince myself I was going to do good on it, but as I was studying the old midterms, I knew I was cooked. There were so many questions, I did not know where to start or even have a chance to get too. 12 coding and probability questions in 50 minutes, stadium pulse was high. I left more questions blank than I liked to and that will end up costing me. The only thing I can say to make myself maybe feel a little-bit better, is I showed up and took the exam. Although I wish I would have spent more time, I will be back in LA before I know it and will have another good time then.
