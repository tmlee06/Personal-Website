This past week was the shortest by far since I had Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday off due to the Golden Week break. I was only in class Thursday and Friday. Thursday I had a Python quiz I totally forgot about, made some rookie mistakes on it, but I am not really mad about it. I am not getting any credit towards my major or credit from UCSD for this class, so this class is purely just practice and playing to win. Since the workload for my courses at Kyoto University is light, I decided to enroll in two online asynchronous courses back at my local community college, EVC (Evergreen Valley College) for the summer. I am taking an ethnic studies course in the Asian American experience to clear my UCSD DEI requirement and an intro to psychology course to clear a prerequisite for a cognitive science course I want to take in the future. By the time I get back to La Jolla, I would have completed my entire general education requirements, and can solely focus on my major courses. 

California Community College system is superb and probably one of the best things about America. Talking with my international suite-mates, I learned that community college and transferring to a 4-year university only exists in America. The concept is so foreign, new, and unorthodox for the other nations. I am so glad I still am able to take CC courses as a UC student. If I am really ambitious I can pick up another associates while completing my bachelors at UCSD. I still have yet to apply for my Mathematics, Physics, and Computer Science associate degrees at EVC and my Data Science one at West Valley College. Although associate degrees do not hold much meaning or power, if it is rare to the rest of the world, and I did the work, might as well get the reward. When I am done with my education, I hope to give back to EVC by becoming a part-time faculty professor. Not only does the job make quite good bank and is a very convenient commute, I get to see the real impact of my work. It is my 生きがい (ikigai), the concept of a perfect balance of professional stability and deeply personal fulfillment. 

Also, I want to point this out. If you are transferring from community college to a UC/CSU, getting IGETC certification will make your transition so much better regardless of major, but do not stress if you do not get certified. I do not regret, not getting certified. If I was IGETC certified, I probably would not have gone abroad to Kyoto, and I do not regret this decision at all. I am probably the most content I have been with myself in over 5+ years, but I have hit a plateau. The honeymoon phase of always being amazed about Japan has passed, this is my life now. It does not mean I hate my life, I am homesick, and want to go home; I am just complacent and completely adjusted to living in Japan. I also hit a plateau in my Japanese language studies (hit that a while ago). The 8-Hour a week course I am taking is not as engaging and helpful as I thought, but who I am to complain as I wipe out my whole Humanities general education requirement for it. The rest of the courses I am taking have little-to-no work at all, attendance is hardly required for lectures, so one can imagine there is not a lot of studying going on. I wish this exchange program was more integrated with Japanese students because after a month in, it is essentially exchange students interacting with each other. The only time I really do interact with other Japanese students is hockey, but when I am there, they just want to practice their English or learn hockey slang. I am essentially Rigby in China:
