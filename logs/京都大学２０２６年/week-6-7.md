Lot of places in the past month, I am very lucky to be able to travel and have great people accompany me with. I had some surprise visitors besides my oldest brother, but I will reveal them later in the main IG post, coming soon. If I have frequent visitors from home, I do not think my "study abroad experience" counts at all 😹. 

Do not get me wrong I am having a blast, but it is just exhausting at times. It is like I am experiencing vacation burnout and aside from going to bed late, I developed some bad habits, been doing some things I am not proud of, a few thou shall not. I cannot seem to kick them, and "I tried and tried and tried 'Til the R switched place with the I."

Really messing with Iceman and felt like that was a clean transition, but I am tired.

You ever feel like there’s a gap between the person you are currently and the person you want and God wants you to be?

I have so much ambition and things I want to do that I know will give me joy, but I just have not been doing them. It is not like I do not want to do the things I love, but I am just not able too. Most of the time when I am not spontaneously adventuring, I just procrastinate. You know I saw this other reel the other day that read something like “if I'm being honest if a doc told me I had 24 hrs to live, I’ll hit a doom-scroll,” and as funny as it is, the scary it is too.

Doom-scrolling and my other bad habits have ruined me, and I do not even want to share my screen time because it is that bad. I hate and do not want to it, but it just happens. It’s like breathing you know, you don’t think when you breathe, you just do it.

Luckily I am conscious of it, and admitting is the first step towards recovery, but it does not necessarily save me. It’s shameful, and I pray to God about it, but as I learned reading <a href="https://www.amazon.com/Overcoming-Unwanted-Intrusive-Thoughts-Frightening/dp/1626254346" style="color: #fffefe; font-weight: bold; text-decoration: underline;">Overcoming Unwanted Intrusive Thoughts: A CBT-Based Guide to Getting Over Frightening, Obsessive, or Disturbing Thoughts</a> by Sally M. Winston and Martin N. Seif, shame is what keeps you going back to your bad habits, and prayers just increase your anxiety. 

I know Matthew 5:30 said, "And if your right hand causes you to stumble, cut it off and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to go into hell." I have done that numerous times with deactivating accounts, but always ended up, and falling back into the cycle. What you resist, persists, and it is not a battle of willpower to stop. It is a neuroscience and dopamine problem. The algorithms are designed to keep you addicted, and it's tough, but in times like these I remember:

Romans 7:21-25:
"So I find this law at work: Although I want to do good, evil is right there with me. For in my inner being I delight in God’s law; but I see another law at work in me, waging war against the law of my mind and making me a prisoner of the law of sin at work within me. What a wretched man I am! Who will rescue me from this body that is subject to death? Thanks be to God, who delivers me through Jesus Christ our Lord!"

But for the first time I had a different feeling when reading that verse and questioned if I even want to be healed. It is exactly what Theo Von is referring to in this clip:

"Do I want to be healed?" I cannot answer that question because truthfully I do not know the answer myself. That's the human struggle, you feel me; part of me wants to be saved and part of me does not. Who I am kidding, I am not even 20 yet, I should not be spiraling into a depressive state of not being the person I want to be just as my life is about to begin. I am making better progress in my self talk and how I view myself though. The other day when I shared my story to new people, I took a moment and smiled at myself for all I accomplished already. My story is actually pretty impressive and insane, and I am happy that I am still here. I am proud and grateful of everything and everyone in my life regardless of good or bad because it shaped who I am. There are some regrets I have, but I think it is good to have some because that is just further proof of your learning and growth. Regretting is confirmation that you are the person who will make the decision now. Although I am still not the 100% fixed version of myself I thought I would before going abroad, I am happy to see a person who tries to their best every time I look in the mirror.
