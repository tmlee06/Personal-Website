An interesting thing that happened last week was that I have got into a lab; Developmental Neurorobotics (Veale Lab) at Kyoto University. The research and projects we do is primarily focused on how eye-movement can be linked to patient’s with schizophrenia. I have no clue how I will be of assistance, but at least I got a cool thing to add to my LinkedIn profile and have something to do abroad other than be a traveling degenerate 😂. Neurotechnology is the field I want to specialize in. When I tell people that, they assume I want to work for Musk’s Neuralink, and want to help him take over people’s minds. If you actually look at what his company does, it’s not at all that. The company’s mission statement is "To create a generalized brain interface to restore autonomy to those with unmet medical needs today and unlock human potential tomorrow." Maybe the last part sounds pretty villainous, but the first half is something I can subscribe to. I strongly believe that with technology, neurological diseases can be cured or at least more livable. Back at UCSD, I am part of Triton Neurotech, and before I came abroad, I was working on a EMG (Electromyography) Neural prosthetic hand. It is powered by a neural wristband like the same type of one Meta uses to control their smart glasses. I think I included a video of it on one of my Instagram posts. The team went to the Neurotech conference in Berkeley, but I have no clue if the project ever got finished or not. If you ever watched Big Hero 6, it is like what the main character presented to get into that university at the conference before the fire broke out, but just not that advanced. I would include the video, but I am writing this log on the plane in the air, and there is no Wi-Fi.

Speaking of Wi-Fi, I must tell you, how much of a blessing, having Wi-Fi, and the invention of Wi-Fi is. My brother and I wasted about an hour trying to get connected to Wi-Fi and get an eSim for the day when we went to Shenzhen. If you never visited China, cellular service is essential because everything runs on WeChat (Weixin). WeChat is everything there. Cash is absolutely obsolete. It is crazy how technology-advanced China is. Shenzhen puts Silicon Valley to shame and the Bay Area is "tech capital of the world." It does make sense that Shenzhen looks more technologically advanced than Silicon Valley because everything coming out of Silicon Valley and what it produces likely came from Shenzhen 😂. Hopefully I get to visit Shenzhen again, but I do not know, I can not keep coming back to the same places over-and-over again because I would be going through Hong Kong. Hong Kong to Shenzhen is 15 minutes via high speed rail, and unlike Japan, the train only cost $10 USD, which is insane. It’s cheaper than BART and Caltrain, and you ride for a shorter duration with better service. Man I am so lucky to be able to travel and see the world. I will announce when it will be up, but for previews and most likely the TLDR will be Shenzhen is peak.
